Republicans go to public colleges more than Democrats

Jay Mahabal

Te Secretary of Education Nominations recently concluded with a historic vote: Vice President Mike Pence broke the tie to appoint Betsy DeVos. There has been a huge whirlwind of controversy, with constituents calling in, etc. etc. Even without this context, the general media narrative has been that Republicans do not care for public education, and that Democrats do.
This is probably accurate, to the extent that it applies to Republicans not caring to fund higher education. However, this should be surprising, because more Republican senators attended public universtities for their Bachelors than Democrats, as a percetnage of their respective groups.
