Where Your Senators Went to College

Jay Mahabal

Donald Trump's Secretary of Education nominations concluded with a historic vote: Vice President Mike Pence had to break a tie in the Senate to appoint Betsy DeVos. This had been a huge whirlwind of controversy, with constituents calling in record numbers to express their displeasure in DeVos' lack of experience, donations to politicians, and stances on a number of education issues. Even without this context, the media narrative for the last few years has been that Republican Party does not care for public education, and that Democrats do.
This is probably more or less accurate, to the extent that it applies to funding higher education. However, this should actually be surprising, because more Republican senators attended public universtities for their Bachelors Degrees than Democrats, as a percentage of their respective groups.
